Regulatory Bodies
Abbreviation Full Name Plain-Language Explanation Source / Body
OFAC Office of Foreign Assets Control U.S. Gov
The U.S. Treasury Department unit that administers and enforces economic and trade sanctions. It maintains the SDN List and issues licenses permitting otherwise-prohibited transactions. Think of OFAC as the "sanctions police" of the United States.
 U.S. Treasury
IRS Internal Revenue Service U.S. Gov
The U.S. federal tax authority. For international purposes, the IRS administers FATCA — requiring foreign financial institutions worldwide to report U.S. account holders or face a 30% withholding tax on U.S.-source payments.
 U.S. Treasury
FinCEN Financial Crimes Enforcement Network U.S. Gov
A U.S. Treasury bureau that collects and analyzes financial intelligence to combat money laundering, terrorist financing, and other financial crimes. In May 2025, FinCEN issued "exceptive relief" allowing U.S. banks to open correspondent accounts with Syrian banks.
 U.S. Treasury
BIS Bureau of Industry and Security U.S. Gov
Part of the U.S. Department of Commerce. BIS controls what goods, software, and technology can be exported from the United States. After E.O. 14312 (June 2025), BIS significantly eased export licensing requirements for civilian-use items going to Syria.
 Commerce Dept.
State Dept. U.S. Department of State U.S. Gov
The U.S. foreign affairs ministry. It designates Foreign Terrorist Organizations (FTOs) and issues waivers under laws like the Caesar Act. The State Department revoked HTS's FTO designation and issued a 180-day Caesar Act waiver in May 2025.
 U.S. Gov
SDN Specially Designated Nationals (and Blocked Persons List) Sanctions
OFAC's master blacklist. Any individual or company on the SDN List has their U.S.-held assets frozen, and U.S. persons are generally prohibited from doing business with them. As of July 1, 2025, 518 Syria-related names were removed — but 139 Assad associates were simultaneously re-added.
 OFAC
E.O. Executive Order Sanctions
A legally binding directive issued by the U.S. President. Syria's sanctions regime was built on six E.O.s (13338, 13399, 13460, 13572, 13573, 13582), all revoked by E.O. 14312 on June 30, 2025. E.O. 13894 was expanded to continue targeting Assad associates.
 White House
GL General License Sanctions
An OFAC authorization that permits a category of otherwise-prohibited transactions without requiring each person to apply individually. GL 24 (Jan 2025) and GL 25 (May 2025) were the two critical licenses that opened Syria transactions before the full sanctions were lifted.
 OFAC
SL Specific License Sanctions
An OFAC license issued to a specific person or company for a specific transaction, when a General License does not cover the situation. Any Syrian government entity whose activities are not covered by GL 25 must apply for an SL directly through OFAC's online portal.
 OFAC
VSD Voluntary Self-Disclosure Sanctions
A process where a company proactively informs OFAC of a potential violation it discovered internally. Filing a VSD typically reduces OFAC penalties by up to 50%. Critical for any company that may have unknowingly violated Syria sanctions before July 1, 2025.
 OFAC
UBO Ultimate Beneficial Owner Sanctions
The real human person(s) who ultimately own or control a company, looking through layers of corporate structures. OFAC's "50% Rule" means: if a sanctioned person owns 50% or more of a company — directly or indirectly — that company is also treated as blocked, even if not named on the SDN List.
 OFAC / FinCEN
CFR Code of Federal Regulations Law
The official compilation of all U.S. federal regulations. Syria's sanctions rules were at 31 CFR Part 542. Following E.O. 14312, OFAC removed Part 542 from the CFR on August 26, 2025, formally erasing the Syria sanctions regulatory framework from U.S. law.
 U.S. Federal
FTO Foreign Terrorist Organization Sanctions
A formal designation by the U.S. State Department that prohibits U.S. persons from providing "material support" to that group. HTS (Hay'at Tahrir al-Sham) held this designation until July 2025, when it was revoked, removing a major barrier to engaging Syria's ruling authority.
 State Dept.
HTS Hay'at Tahrir al-Sham Sanctions
The Syrian armed group that led the overthrow of Assad in December 2024 and now forms the core of Syria's transitional government under President Ahmed al-Sharaa. Its FTO designation was revoked in July 2025, enabling legal engagement with Syria's government.
 State Dept.
PAARSS Promoting Accountability for Assad and Regional Stabilization Sanctions Sanctions
The renamed sanctions program (formerly Syria-Related Sanctions Regulations) under E.O. 13894, as expanded by E.O. 14312. This is the surviving sanctions framework targeting Assad associates, human rights abusers, Captagon traffickers, and destabilizing regional actors.
 OFAC
NDAA National Defense Authorization Act Law
The U.S. annual defense budget legislation. The FY 2026 NDAA (signed December 18, 2025) included §8369, which fully repealed the Caesar Act and set ongoing 180-day presidential certification conditions Syria must meet to maintain sanction-free status.
 U.S. Congress
BSA Bank Secrecy Act U.S. Law
The foundational U.S. anti-money laundering law (1970). It requires financial institutions doing business with the U.S. to maintain AML programs, file Currency Transaction Reports (CTRs), and submit Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs) to FinCEN. Essential for Syrian banks seeking U.S. correspondent banking access.
 FinCEN
SAR Suspicious Activity Report Banking
A mandatory report filed by financial institutions when they detect a transaction that may involve money laundering, fraud, or other financial crimes. In Syria, Legislative Decree 27 (2011) requires filing SARs with Syria's AML/CTF Authority. U.S. correspondent banks require Syrian partners to have a functioning SAR regime.
 FinCEN / FATF
FATCA Foreign Account Tax Compliance Act Tax
A 2010 U.S. law (part of the HIRE Act) requiring all foreign financial institutions worldwide to identify and report accounts held by U.S. taxpayers to the IRS — or face a 30% withholding tax on U.S.-source payments. Every Syrian bank must now comply. Syria currently has no IGA with the U.S., making direct registration the only path.
 IRS / Treasury
IGA Intergovernmental Agreement Tax
A bilateral treaty between a foreign country and the United States to implement FATCA. A Model 1 IGA (recommended for Syria) lets Syrian banks report U.S. account data to Syria's tax authority, which then shares it with the IRS — far simpler than each bank reporting directly. Over 115 countries have signed IGAs.
 U.S. Treasury / IRS
GIIN Global Intermediary Identification Number Tax
A unique registration number assigned by the IRS to foreign financial institutions that register under FATCA. Without a GIIN, a Syrian bank will be subject to 30% withholding on any U.S.-source payments it receives. Registration is free, done online, and is the single most urgent tax compliance step.
 IRS
FFI Foreign Financial Institution Tax
FATCA's term for any non-U.S. bank, investment fund, insurance company, or other financial entity. Syrian banks, insurance companies, and investment firms are all FFIs under FATCA and must register with the IRS, obtain a GIIN, and comply with reporting obligations.
 IRS / FATCA
RO Responsible Officer (FATCA) Tax
A senior officer of a foreign financial institution who is personally responsible for the entity's FATCA compliance and certifications to the IRS. The RO must complete periodic certifications and cannot fully delegate this responsibility. Appointment of an RO is mandatory for all FATCA-registered Syrian financial institutions.
 IRS / FATCA
CRS Common Reporting Standard Tax
The OECD's global equivalent of FATCA — a multilateral automatic exchange of financial account information between over 100 countries. While FATCA is U.S.-specific, CRS covers all participating countries. Syria will eventually need to adopt CRS as part of international financial integration.
 OECD
HIRE Act Hiring Incentives to Restore Employment Act Tax
The 2010 U.S. law that created FATCA. Knowing its origin is useful context: FATCA was primarily a tax-gap-closing measure, not a sanctions tool — which is why it applies to Syria even after sanctions were lifted. Any Syrian FFI with U.S. account holders must comply regardless of sanctions status.
 U.S. Congress
IRC Internal Revenue Code Tax
The body of U.S. federal tax law. FATCA obligations are found in IRC §§1471–1474. These sections define who must comply, what must be reported, and the 30% withholding penalty for non-compliance. Referenced in all FATCA legal documentation.
 U.S. Law
AML Anti-Money Laundering Finance
A set of laws, regulations, and procedures designed to prevent criminals from disguising illegally obtained funds as legitimate income. AML programs are mandatory for all financial institutions. Syrian banks must have robust AML programs to access international correspondent banking and satisfy FATF requirements.
 FATF / FinCEN
CFT Counter-Financing of Terrorism Finance
Measures designed to prevent funds from reaching terrorist organizations. Always paired with AML in regulatory frameworks (written as "AML/CFT"). FATF Recommendations 5–8 specifically address CFT. A strong CFT program is critical for Syria to demonstrate it is not financing designated groups like ISIS.
 FATF / UN
KYC Know Your Customer Finance
The mandatory process by which financial institutions verify the identity of their clients before and during a banking relationship. KYC includes collecting ID documents, understanding the source of funds, and assessing risk. International banks will require Syrian institutions to demonstrate strong KYC before opening accounts.
 FATF / Basel
CDD Customer Due Diligence Finance
A more formal term for KYC processes. CDD includes identifying the customer, verifying their identity, understanding the nature of their business, and ongoing monitoring. "Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)" applies to high-risk customers such as Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs) — common in the Syrian government context.
 FATF Rec. 10
PEP Politically Exposed Person Finance
An individual who holds or has held a prominent public position (e.g., government minister, military commander, state company executive) or is closely related to one. PEPs are considered higher money-laundering risk and require Enhanced Due Diligence. Senior Syrian government officials and directors of government-owned companies will be treated as PEPs by international banks.
 FATF Rec. 12
NRA National Risk Assessment Finance
A formal assessment conducted by a country of its own money laundering and terrorist financing risks, using FATF's methodology. Syria must prepare and submit an NRA as part of the FATF on-site evaluation process. A strong NRA demonstrates the country understands its risk landscape and has proportionate controls.
 FATF
IFC International Finance Corporation Finance
The private-sector lending arm of the World Bank Group. IFC provides loans, equity, and advisory services to private businesses in developing countries. It is a key financing source for private Syrian companies and public-private partnerships. IFC requires strong governance and AML/CFT compliance from borrowers.
 World Bank Group
IsDB Islamic Development Bank Finance
A multilateral development bank headquartered in Jeddah that provides Shariah-compliant financing (no conventional interest) to Muslim-majority countries. IsDB is a natural financing partner for Syria's reconstruction. Membership and access requires demonstrated political stability and financial governance reforms.
 Multilateral
IBRD International Bank for Reconstruction and Development Finance
The original "World Bank" — provides loans to middle-income countries for development projects. Syria's $216 billion reconstruction need will require IBRD engagement. Access requires OFAC and FATCA compliance, and no outstanding sanctions against the borrowing government entity.
 World Bank Group
IDA International Development Association Finance
The World Bank's fund for the poorest countries, providing grants and zero- or low-interest loans. Syria, given its post-war economic devastation, may qualify for IDA financing — which is even more favorable than IBRD terms. Requires government reform benchmarks to be met.
 World Bank Group
FATF Financial Action Task Force International
The global standard-setter for AML/CFT and counter-proliferation financing. FATF's 40 Recommendations are the internationally accepted framework. Countries are rated on compliance and placed on a "white list" (compliant), "gray list" (increased monitoring — where Syria has been since 2010), or "black list" (high-risk). Gray-listing causes banks worldwide to apply much stricter scrutiny to Syrian transactions.
 FATF (Paris)
MENAFATF Middle East and North Africa Financial Action Task Force International
The FATF-style regional body for the MENA region, of which Syria is a member. MENAFATF coordinates AML/CFT evaluations and technical assistance in the region. Syria's engagement with MENAFATF is a prerequisite for the FATF on-site evaluation that would clear Syria from the gray list.
 FATF / Regional
OECD Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development International
An international organization of 38 countries that sets global economic, tax, and financial standards. The OECD created the CRS (Common Reporting Standard) — FATCA's global counterpart. OECD membership is a long-term goal that signals full economic normalization for any recovering country.
 International
UN United Nations International
The global intergovernmental organization. The UN Security Council maintains its own sanctions lists separate from U.S. OFAC lists. All AML/CFT screening systems must check UN lists in addition to OFAC and EU lists. Some Syria-related UN sanctions (e.g., on ISIS, Al-Qaeda) remain fully in force regardless of U.S. relief.
 International
EU European Union International
The political and economic union of 27 European nations. The EU maintained its own Syria sanctions program separate from U.S. sanctions. The EU lifted the majority of its Syria sanctions from May 28, 2025, but retained measures on military items, surveillance technology, and cultural property. Syrian companies transacting with EU persons must check both OFAC and EU lists.
 EU Council
MLA Mutual Legal Assistance International
A process through which countries formally request and provide assistance to each other in criminal investigations and proceedings — including sharing financial evidence, freezing assets, and extraditing suspects. Syria improved its MLA framework under Legislative Decree 27 (2011). A functioning MLA framework is required under FATF Recommendations 36–38.
 FATF / UN
SDF Syrian Democratic Forces Syria
The Kurdish-led armed group controlling parts of northeastern Syria. The March 10, 2025 agreement between the Syrian government and the SDF is one of the conditions the U.S. President must certify to Congress every 180 days (per NDAA §8369) to maintain Syria's sanctions-free status. Failure to implement this agreement could trigger sanctions reimposition.
 Syria / U.S. State
CCO Chief Compliance Officer Governance
The senior executive responsible for overseeing and managing compliance with laws, regulations, and internal policies across an organization. In the context of this roadmap, the CCO leads the Internal Compliance Committee, oversees OFAC screening, AML/CFT programs, and coordinates with legal counsel. Every major Syrian government-owned company should appoint a CCO.
 Corporate Gov.
ICC Internal Compliance Committee Governance
The dedicated internal body established within an organization (here: the Syrian government) to oversee sanctions compliance, AML/CFT obligations, and FATCA reporting. In this roadmap, the ICC should include the CCO, AML Officer, FATCA Responsible Officer, and General Counsel. It reports directly to the Board of Members.
 Internal / Best Practice
CTR Currency Transaction Report Finance
A mandatory report filed by U.S. financial institutions with FinCEN for any cash transaction over $10,000. For Syrian banks seeking U.S. correspondent relationships, understanding and adopting equivalent reporting thresholds under BSA requirements demonstrates AML compliance credibility.
 FinCEN / BSA
PPP Public-Private Partnership Governance
A cooperative arrangement between government and private sector companies to finance, build, and operate public services or infrastructure. PPP structures are commonly used in reconstruction efforts. For Syrian government-owned companies, PPP arrangements with foreign investors (now permitted post-E.O. 14312) can accelerate reconstruction while sharing financial risk.
 Finance / Gov.
EDD Enhanced Due Diligence Finance
A higher level of scrutiny applied to high-risk customers, transactions, or jurisdictions. International banks will apply EDD to any Syrian government entity or official given Syria's recent sanctions history and FATF gray-list status. EDD involves deeper investigation into source of funds, ownership structures, and business purpose.
 FATF Rec. 19
CBW Act Chemical and Biological Weapons Control and Warfare Elimination Act Law
A U.S. law imposing sanctions and export restrictions on countries found to have used chemical or biological weapons. E.O. 14312 waived certain CBW Act provisions relating to Syria (foreign assistance, U.S. government credit, financial institution loans). However, Syria must demonstrate it is not pursuing chemical or biological weapons programs to maintain this waiver.
 U.S. Congress
SySR Syrian Sanctions Regulations Sanctions
The now-defunct U.S. regulatory framework at 31 CFR Part 542 that implemented Syria's comprehensive sanctions. Revoked by E.O. 14312 effective July 1, 2025, and formally removed from the Code of Federal Regulations on August 26, 2025. Understanding this term is important because any violation that occurred before July 1, 2025 under the SySR may still be investigated by OFAC.
 OFAC (revoked)
